## Overview

Minimum wage rates continue to change across the country. Multiple states and localities have enacted increases, and employers must ensure they are paying the correct rate in every jurisdiction where they have employees.

## Key Changes

Several states adjust their minimum wage annually based on consumer price index calculations. Others have enacted scheduled increases as part of phased legislation. Employers with multi-state operations should review rates in each jurisdiction at least quarterly.

## What Employers Should Do

1. **Audit current pay rates** against the applicable minimum wage in each location
2. **Update payroll systems** to reflect new rates before the effective dates
3. **Review exempt salary thresholds** that may also be changing
4. **Post updated notices** as required by state and federal law
5. **Communicate changes** to affected employees

## Looking Ahead

The trend toward higher minimum wages at the state and local level is expected to continue. Employers should budget for potential increases and monitor legislative developments in all jurisdictions where they operate.
